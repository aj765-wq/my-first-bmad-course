# Session 3: Common Mistakes to Avoid When Caring for Indoor Plants

Hey, plant pals! We've covered the basics of choosing easy plants and watering them right. Now, let's talk about the pitfalls that trip up even experienced plant owners. By avoiding these common mistakes, you'll set your indoor garden up for success. Let's dive in!

## Top Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

- **Overwatering**: This is the number one killer! Signs include yellow leaves and mushy stems. Solution: Always check soil moisture before watering and ensure good drainage.
- **Wrong Light Levels**: Too much direct sun can scorch leaves, while too little causes leggy growth. Tip: Research your plant's light needs and place accordingly – most prefer bright, indirect light.
- **Ignoring Pests**: Tiny bugs like spider mites or aphids can sneak in. Prevention: Inspect plants regularly and quarantine new ones. Use neem oil spray if needed.
- **Using the Wrong Soil**: Heavy garden soil can compact and suffocate roots. Fix: Use potting mix designed for indoor plants – it's lighter and drains better.
- **Not Repotting**: Roots can get cramped, leading to stunted growth. Rule: Repot every 1-2 years or when roots poke out the bottom.

## Signs of Trouble

- Wilting leaves: Could be underwatering or overwatering
- Brown tips: Often low humidity or fluoride in tap water
- Dropping leaves: Stress from temperature changes or drafts

## Practical Example

Suppose your Snake Plant starts getting yellow leaves. Instead of panicking, check if you've been overwatering. Snake Plants prefer to dry out between waterings. Adjust your routine, and it should perk up. If the problem persists, it might be root rot – gently remove from the pot and trim any mushy roots before repotting in fresh soil.

## Summary

Avoiding overwatering, providing the right light, watching for pests, using proper soil, and repotting when needed will keep your plants healthy. Remember, plants are resilient – if you catch issues early, most can be fixed. You've got this! With these tips, your indoor plants will be thriving in no time. Happy planting!
