# Overcoming Presentation Anxiety
## Quick Reference Handout for Graduate Students

**Remember Oprah:** Even the most successful speakers start shy. Practice builds confidence!

---

## Why We Get Nervous
- Body's natural response to stress
- Heart races, palms sweat, mind blanks
- Normal for thesis defense - even professors feel it
- High stakes + expert audience = extra pressure

---

## 3 Techniques to Try Right Now

### 1. Reframe Anxiety as Excitement
- Notice symptoms (racing heart, butterflies)
- Say: "This is excitement about my research!"
- Smile and breathe deeply
- Channel energy into confident delivery

### 2. Power Pose Warm-Up
- Stand tall, feet apart, hands on hips
- Hold for 2 minutes, breathe deeply
- Builds confidence through body language
- Increases confidence hormones

### 3. Worst-Case Scenario Check
- Write down your biggest fear
- Ask: "If this happens, what next?"
- Focus on what you control
- Accept imperfection

---

## 4-Step Process
```
NOTICE symptoms → PAUSE and breathe → CHOOSE excitement → ACT confidently
```

---

## 2-Minute Breathing Reset
- Sit comfortably, hands on chest/belly
- Inhale 4 counts (nose), hold 2, exhale 6 (mouth)
- Repeat 8-10 times
- Use as your "reset button"

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't try to eliminate anxiety completely
- Don't over-prepare without practicing out loud
- Don't avoid eye contact

---

## Key Takeaways
- Anxiety and excitement feel the same - choose excitement
- Your body affects your mind - use power poses
- Focus on what you control - preparation and delivery

---

## Your Action Items This Week
- Practice reframing before next presentation
- Try power pose daily
- Do breathing exercise 2x daily
- Share defense date with accountability partner
- Start small, build confidence gradually

**You've got this! Your research matters.**
