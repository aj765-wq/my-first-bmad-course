# Overcoming Presentation Anxiety
## 20-Minute Session for Graduate Students Preparing for Thesis Defense

**Speaker Notes:** Welcome everyone! Today we're tackling one of the biggest challenges graduate students face - presentation anxiety. We'll cover practical techniques, real stories, and tools you can use immediately. Remember to pause after each technique to let students practice briefly.

---

# Oprah's Story: From Shy to Confident

- Oprah Winfrey was terrified of public speaking as a child
- Teachers sent notes home saying she was "too quiet"
- She practiced in front of mirrors and joined speech club
- Built confidence gradually through consistent effort
- Now speaks to millions without fear

**Speaker Notes:** Start with this inspiring story to show that even the most successful people start somewhere. Ask: "If Oprah can overcome her fear, what about you?" This sets a positive, achievable tone for the session.

---

# Introduction: That Nervous Feeling

- We all get nervous before presentations
- Heart racing, sweaty palms, mind going blank
- Especially tough for thesis defense
- But anxiety is normal and can be managed
- Today: why it happens + 3 techniques + breathing exercise

**Speaker Notes:** Make this conversational. Ask the group: "Have you ever felt your heart racing just thinking about presenting?" Share that you're here to help them turn that anxiety into confidence. Keep energy high and supportive.

---

# Why We Feel Nervous

- Body's natural "fight or flight" response
- Heart beats faster, breathing gets shallow
- Hands shake, thoughts race
- For thesis defense: high stakes, expert audience
- Good news: this is normal and manageable

**Speaker Notes:** Explain this simply - no jargon. Use hand gestures to show physical symptoms. Emphasize that even professors feel this way. Transition: "But we can work with our body's response instead of against it."

---

# Technique 1: Reframe Anxiety as Excitement

- Anxiety and excitement feel the same physically
- Racing heart, butterflies in stomach
- Choose to see symptoms as excitement about your research
- Take a deep breath and smile
- Channel energy into your delivery

**Speaker Notes:** This is a game-changer! Have students practice saying: "This is excitement about sharing my research!" Demonstrate the smile and deep breath. Research shows this reduces anxiety by up to 50%.

---

# Technique 2: Power Pose Warm-Up

- Body language affects your mindset
- Stand tall, feet apart, hands on hips (Wonder Woman pose)
- Hold for 2 minutes while breathing deeply
- Builds confidence through posture
- Increases confidence hormones, decreases stress hormones

**Speaker Notes:** Show the pose yourself! It's fun and immediate. Tell them: "Try this in a bathroom stall before your defense." Studies show 2 minutes increases testosterone by 20%. Have them practice briefly now.

---

# Technique 3: Worst-Case Scenario Reality Check

- Write down your biggest fear about the defense
- Ask: "If this happened, what would I do next?"
- Realize worst outcome isn't career-ending
- Focus on what you can control: preparation and delivery
- Let go of needing everything to be perfect

**Speaker Notes:** This grounds anxious thoughts. Share that most "worst-case" scenarios are survivable. Ask students to think of their biggest fear silently for 30 seconds. Emphasize: "You've earned your place at the table."

- Severe anxiety before team presentations
- Used power poses and reframing daily
- Gradually presented to larger groups

**Speaker Notes:** Tech example for STEM students. Show that these techniques work in corporate settings too. Ask: "If a senior engineer at Google can do this, what about you?" Keep it brief but impactful.

# 4-Step Process to Overcome Anxiety

```
Step 1: NOTICE → Step 2: PAUSE → Step 3: CHOOSE → Step 4: ACT
```

- Notice physical symptoms when they appear
- Pause and take a deep breath
- Choose to reframe as excitement
- Act with confidence using the techniques

**Speaker Notes:** Draw this simple diagram on a whiteboard or show it visually. Walk through each step slowly. This gives them a clear framework to remember. Practice the sequence as a group.

---

# 2-Minute Breathing Exercise

- Sit comfortably, hand on chest and belly
- Breathe in for 4 counts through nose
- Hold for 2 counts
- Breathe out for 6 counts through mouth
- Repeat for 2 minutes (8-10 cycles)

**Speaker Notes:** Lead them through this now! Count out loud: "In 2-3-4, hold 2, out 2-3-4-5-6." This becomes their "reset button." Encourage daily practice. Notice how the room feels calmer afterward.

---

# Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Don't try to eliminate anxiety completely
- Don't over-prepare without practicing delivery
- Don't avoid eye contact with your audience
- Accept anxiety as normal energy
- Rehearse out loud and make brief connections

**Speaker Notes:** These are crucial learnings! Share that trying to be "perfect" often makes anxiety worse. Ask: "Which of these have you done?" Turn mistakes into learning opportunities.

---

# Key Takeaways

- Anxiety and excitement feel the same - choose excitement
- Your body affects your mind - use power poses
- Focus on what you control - preparation and delivery
- Practice daily, start small
- You've earned your place at the table

**Speaker Notes:** Summarize the three main techniques. End with encouragement: "You've got this! Your research matters." Mention next session on structuring presentations. Ask for one thing they'll try this week.
