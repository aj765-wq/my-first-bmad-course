# Introduction to Time Management - Course Outline

## Course Overview
- **Target Audience:** University Students
- **Total Duration:** 1 hour (3 sessions)
- **Format:** Interactive Workshop Series

---

## Session 1: Why Time Management Matters
**Duration:** 20 minutes

### Key Topics:
1. **The Impact of Poor Time Management**
   - How procrastination affects academic performance
   - Stress and burnout in student life
   - Connection between time management and mental health

2. **Benefits of Effective Time Management**
   - Increased productivity and academic success
   - Better work-life balance
   - Reduced anxiety and improved well-being

3. **Common Time-Wasters for Students**
   - Social media and digital distractions
   - Multitasking myths
   - Perfectionism and analysis paralysis

4. **Setting the Foundation**
   - Understanding personal time management styles
   - Identifying your peak productivity hours

---

## Session 2: The Main Techniques
**Duration:** 20 minutes

### Key Topics:
1. **Priority Management Methods**
   - The Eisenhower Matrix (Urgent vs. Important)
   - ABC prioritization method
   - Identifying high-impact tasks

2. **Time-Blocking Strategies**
   - Creating structured daily schedules
   - Time blocking for classes, study, and personal time
   - The Pomodoro Technique for focused work

3. **Planning and Goal-Setting**
   - SMART goals framework
   - Breaking large projects into manageable tasks
   - Weekly and daily planning rituals

4. **Tools and Technology**
   - Digital calendar systems
   - Task management apps and tools
   - Choosing the right system for your needs

---

## Session 3: Creating Your Plan
**Duration:** 20 minutes

### Key Topics:
1. **Personal Assessment**
   - Analyzing your current schedule
   - Identifying time leaks and inefficiencies
   - Recognizing your unique challenges and strengths

2. **Building Your Time Management System**
   - Designing a personalized daily routine
   - Creating a semester-long academic calendar
   - Integrating your chosen techniques and tools

3. **Implementation and Accountability**
   - Getting started with your plan
   - Tracking progress and adjusting as needed
   - Building accountability partners or study groups

4. **Sustaining Long-Term Success**
   - Avoiding common pitfalls in habit formation
   - Regular review and refinement
   - Resources for continued learning and support

---

## Course Materials & Resources
- Downloadable time management worksheets
- Recommended apps and tools (digital calendar, task managers)
- Templates for weekly and daily planning
- Resource list for additional support
